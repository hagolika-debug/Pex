A tiny borderland between New Hampshire and Canada sparked a hidden war. The 1783 Treaty of Paris left the exact boundary ambiguous[1s]. Double taxes from both nations angered settlers[0.5s], fueling a push for self‐rule. On July 9 1832, they declared the Republic of Indian Stream. Luther Parker drafted a constitution, creating a tiny democratic government. Militia from New Hampshire gathered, demanding the land’s surrender.[1s]Tension rose. By August 5 1835, the republic quietly ceded to New Hampshire. The border dispute finally ended with the 1842 Webster‐Ashburton Treaty. Today, a historic marker commemorates the forgotten nation’s brief existence.
