In 1900, Michigan's hidden tragedy unfolded at Burt Lake. Sheriff Fred Ming led deputies to torch an entire Indigenous village. Land speculator John McGinn claimed tax titles to seize the land. Nineteen families watched their homes burn while the church survived. The burn-out erased a century‐old Ottawa‐Chippewa settlement overnight. Treaties of 1836 and 1855 had promised this land forever. Legal battles continued for decades, yet justice remained elusive. Today, descendants remember the fire as a scarred memory. Burt Lake Burn‐Out reminds us of forgotten histories demanding recognition.
