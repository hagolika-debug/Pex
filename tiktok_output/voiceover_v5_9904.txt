In 897 AD, Rome witnessed a trial unlike any other. Pope Stephen VI ordered the exhumation of his predecessor. The corpse of Pope Formosus rose from the tomb[1s], still rotting. Clad in papal robes, the dead pope was placed on a throne. A deacon[0.5s] answered for him, chanting accusations of perjury and corruption. The silent courtroom echoed with the clang of chains and judgment. Finally[0.8s], Stephen declared Formosus guilty, stripping him of papal symbols. The corpse was torn apart, its remains cast into the Tiber River. The macabre verdict sparked riots[1.2s], leading to Stephen’s own brutal downfall.
