In 1788, an entire army fought itself—no enemy in sight. The Austrian troops marched toward Caransebeș, chasing phantom Turkish forces. A drunken hussar refused sharing schnapps, sparking a heated argument. A single shot rang out, and chaos erupted instantly.[1s] Confused infantry shouted 'Turcii! Turcii!', believing Ottoman cavalry approached.[0.8s] Orders misheard: 'Halt! Halt!' became 'Allah! Allah!' in panic.[0.5s] General Colloredo ordered artillery fire, thinking Ottoman cavalry charged. The night turned into a self‐destructive massacre, casualties unknown. Two days later, real Turks arrived, finding a decimated camp. History calls it the worst friendly‐fire disaster ever recorded.
