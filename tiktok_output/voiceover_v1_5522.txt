Imagine meat falling from the sky—yes, it actually happened in 1876. On March 3rd, a quiet Kentucky farm became a bizarre battlefield. Mrs. Crouch was making soap when chunks of red meat crashed down. Pieces the size of fists rained over a 100‐by‐50‐yard field. The New York Times and Scientific American reported the strange ‘meat rain’. Scientists guessed vulture vomit, cyanobacteria, or even cosmic meat. Later labs identified the chunks as animal tissue—lung, muscle, cartilage. Kentucky now celebrates the event with a quirky festival and museum exhibit. In 2024, a preserved sample resurfaced at Transylvania University for study. So next time it rains, hope it’s just water—not steak!
