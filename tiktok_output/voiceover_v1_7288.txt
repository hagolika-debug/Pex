One woman survived three sister ships that all sank. In 1911, stewardess Violet Jessop boarded the massive RMS Olympic. A naval collision tore through its hull, but she escaped. She transferred to the Titanic just months before its voyage. When icebergs struck, she calmly boarded a lifeboat and survived. During WWI, she volunteered as a nurse on the Britannic. A hidden mine detonated, throwing her into freezing Aegean waters. She kicked away from the propeller, swam to safety, and lived. Violet outlived every vessel, proving human resilience beats steel.
