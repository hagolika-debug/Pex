A forgotten nation lived between the US and Canada for three years. In 1832,[0.8s] 56 locals voted for independence, drafting a unique constitution. They created a council, militia, and tax system for 400 residents. But neighboring nations ignored them, demanding taxes and military service. A New Hampshire sheriff tried collecting debts, sparking armed standoff. Finally,[1s] on August 5, 1835, militia threatened invasion. The tiny republic surrendered, becoming part of New Hampshire again. The border[0.5s] finally settled in 1842 by the Webster‐Ashburton Treaty.
