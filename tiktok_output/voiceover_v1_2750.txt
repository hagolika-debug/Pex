In 1839, Missouri and Iowa almost went to war over a border. A vague phrase about ‘rapids’ sparked a dispute over a nine‐mile strip. Missouri tax collectors entered Iowa, demanding money and cutting down honey‐filled trees. Iowa locals grabbed pitchforks, chased away the collectors, and seized a sheriff. Both states mobilized militias, but the standoff remained bloodless and comedic. The conflict escalated to a face‐off with whiskey‐laden wagons on the border. Eventually, the U.S. Supreme Court settled the border in Iowa’s favor. Today, markers stand where soldiers once stood, reminding us of the Honey War. A quirky piece of history showing how a honey harvest almost sparked a war.
