In the forests of New Hampshire, a tiny nation emerged. A vague 1783 treaty left its border in perpetual confusion. On July 9, 1832, they declared independence.[1s] The world ignored them. They drafted a constitution, elected a council, and levied taxes. Both British and American sheriffs taxed them.[0.8s] The citizens rebelled. New Hampshire militia marched, demanding surrender; the tiny republic trembled. By August 5, 1835, they signed over to New Hampshire. The land became Pittsburg, but the story fades. A forgotten republic, born from a map error.[1.2s] Remember it?
