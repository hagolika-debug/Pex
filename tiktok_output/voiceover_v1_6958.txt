April 1945, a secret mission saved priceless horses from war. Hostau, Czechoslovakia: a hidden farm holding the legendary Lipizzaners. German vets feared Soviet troops would slaughter the pregnant mares. American 42nd Cavalry received a daring order: rescue the herd. They rode into enemy lines, guns silent[1s], hearts pounding. Allied and German soldiers fought side by side against the Waffen-SS. Over 300 horses, many unborn, were herded onto trucks. The last Soviet tank appeared[0.8s], but they escaped. Operation Cowboy became a hidden triumph of compassion in war. Today, those Lipizzaners grace Vienna's grand performances, thanks to bravery.
