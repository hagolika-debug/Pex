In August 1835, New York’s Sun printed impossible lunar discoveries.[0.8s] The articles claimed Sir John Herschel found bison, unicorns, and bat‐men on the Moon.[1s] A massive 24‐foot telescope supposedly captured the alien wildlife from South Africa. Readers were mesmerized, buying copies by the thousands each day.[1.2s] Scientists scoffed, but the public craved sensational space news. The hoax ran for six weeks before The Sun reluctantly admitted the lie.[1s] The scandal boosted circulation dramatically, cementing the era’s yellow journalism. Edgar Allan Poe later parodied the lunar saga, mocking its absurdity. Today the Great Moon Hoax warns how fiction can masquerade as fact.[1.3s]
