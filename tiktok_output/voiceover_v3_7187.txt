The 1904 Olympic marathon was less a race than a survival nightmare. Runners faced hundred-degree heat, choking dust, and exactly one water station. Coaches handed out strychnine, brandy, and raw eggs to keep them moving. Fred Lorz hitched a car for eleven miles, then jogged to fake victory. Thomas Hicks collapsed, hallucinating, and was literally carried across the finish line. Two South African runners were forced off course by aggressive stray dogs. Only fourteen of thirty-two finished, and the winner nearly died from exhaustion. Sports history’s craziest day proves human endurance has always been beautifully unhinged.
