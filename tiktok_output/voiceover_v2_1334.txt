A single pig sparked a war between two global superpowers. In 1859, an American farmer shot a British pig trespassing on his crops. The British demanded compensation, but the farmer refused to pay a penny. Tensions exploded, sending warships and hundreds of soldiers to a tiny island. For thirteen tense weeks, cannons aimed at each other across a quiet field. Diplomats finally intervened, negotiating a peaceful joint occupation instead of bloodshed. The Pig War ended without a single human casualty, proving diplomacy wins.
