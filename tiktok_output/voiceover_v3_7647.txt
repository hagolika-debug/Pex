In 1859, the sky turned blood red and telegraphs sparked. Astronomer Richard Carrington watched a massive solar flare erupt. Within hours, Earth’s magnetic field collapsed under the storm. Telegraph operators got shocked as machines caught fire spontaneously. Auroras lit up the Caribbean, Cuba, and Hawaii. If it hit today, satellites would fry instantly. Trillions in damage, global blackouts, and silent cities. Space weather is real, and it’s already brewing.
