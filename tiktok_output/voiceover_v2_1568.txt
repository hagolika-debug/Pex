In the early 1800s, a forgotten nation sprang between the US and Canada. A vague border from the 1783 Treaty of Paris left both sides confused. Residents faced double taxes, prompting them to declare independence on July 9, 1832.[1s] They drafted a constitution, naming their land the Indian Stream Republic. The tiny nation lasted three years, until New Hampshire militia threatened invasion. A hardware-store debt sparked a bizarre shootout with British deputies.[0.5s] Both governments protested, fearing war over a trivial $10 dispute. Finally, the 1842 Webster‐Ashburton Treaty settled the border peacefully.[1s] Today, the story lives in a historic marker in Pittsburg, New Hampshire.
