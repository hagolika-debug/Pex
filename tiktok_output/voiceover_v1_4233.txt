January 10, 1920, Siberia—American troops stumble into an ambush.[1s] Only 39 men, barely armed, faced a furious Cossack assault. The Cossacks rode armored trains, guns blazing, expecting easy victory.[0.8s] But the platoon held the line, exchanging fire from frozen trenches. Two soldiers fell, two wounded, yet the Americans repelled the attackers. Their stubborn defense forced the Cossacks to retreat, ending the battle. This skirmish marked the final American combat in the Russian Civil War. Soon after, the U.S. expedition withdrew, leaving Siberia's icy silence. The Battle of Posolskeya remains a forgotten footnote in history.
