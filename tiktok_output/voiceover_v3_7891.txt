In 1838, Maine and British New Brunswick nearly went to war. A vague 1783 treaty left the border unclear, sparking timber battles. Soldiers survived on simple pork and beans, giving the clash its nickname. Local militias mustered, yet no real combat ever erupted. The infamous Battle of Caribou erupted when a bear startled troops. Despite tensions, there were zero battle deaths—only 38 non‐combat losses. Lord Ashburton and Daniel Webster negotiated the 1842 Webster‐Ashburton Treaty. The treaty awarded most of the disputed land to Maine, ending the feud. Today, the quirky Republic of Madawaska lives on in local folklore. A war won without a single shot—history’s oddest peaceful victory.
