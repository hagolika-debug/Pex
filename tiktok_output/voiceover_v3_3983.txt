In the 1830s, a tiny nation blossomed between the US and Canada. The border dispute stemmed from the 1783 Treaty of Paris.[0.5s] Both Britain and America claimed the land, sending tax collectors. Fed up, locals declared independence on July 9, 1832. They drafted a constitution, electing Luther Parker as justice.[1s] The Republic lasted three years, surviving double taxation and militia threats. In August 1835, New Hampshire militia forced annexation.[0.8s] The tiny state vanished, but its story lives in Pittsburg. A forgotten footnote reminds us borders are human lines, not destiny.
