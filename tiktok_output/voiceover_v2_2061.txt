In 1864, Pennsylvania's mountains hid a secret draft rebellion. Union drafts forced men, sparking massive evasion across Columbia County. Deserters formed the Lincoln Midnight Riders to hunt fellow draft‐evaders. Rumors spread of a hidden fort bristling with cannons on North Mountain. Governor dispatched nearly a thousand troops to crush the imagined uprising. Soldiers trekked the forest, finding only logging cabins and shingle piles. Over a hundred locals arrested; forty‐four sent to Fort Mifflin. General Cadwalader later called the whole affair a grand farce. Today a marker stands, remembering this forgotten Civil War drama.
