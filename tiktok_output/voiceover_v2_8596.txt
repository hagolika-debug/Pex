In 1859, a single pig nearly started a war between America and Britain. A British farmer shot an American settler's pig wandering into his garden. The settler demanded compensation, but the farmer refused to pay anything. Both sides called their governments, and soon troops arrived on the island. Within weeks, hundreds of armed men faced off across a muddy field. Cannons were loaded, orders were drafted, yet nobody actually wanted to fire. For months, the two armies shared whiskey and played cards together. Finally, diplomats negotiated a peaceful joint occupation instead of a bloody conflict. The only casualty was that one unfortunate pig, forever remembered in history.
