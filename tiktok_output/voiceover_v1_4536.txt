In 1839, Missouri and Iowa nearly went to war. A faulty survey misplaced the Des Moines Rapids, sparking confusion. Missouri sheriff cut down three honey‐laden trees for unpaid taxes. Iowa courts fined the Missouri farmer, igniting the feud. Both governors issued arrest warrants,[0.8s] mobilizing local militias. Hundreds gathered at the border.[1s] Armed with pitchforks and whiskey. Missouri sheriff Gregory was seized and jailed in Muscatine. A standoff ensued.[1.5s] No shots were fired. The Supreme Court finally drew the line, ending the Honey War.
