In March 1876, Kentucky witnessed a rain unlike any other.[1s] Red chunks of meat fell from a clear sky, covering the fields. Mrs. Crouch, soap‐making on her porch, stared at the falling flesh. The strange shower lasted only minutes, spreading over a 100‐by‐50‐yard area. Neighbors claimed it was a divine sign, others whispered of a cursed omen.[1s] Scientists argued: vulture vomit, cyanobacteria blooms, or even cosmic meat? Modern consensus points to startled vultures disgorging partially digested meat mid‐flight.[0.5s] A sealed sample survived, rediscovered in 2004 inside Transylvania University’s archives. Now Bath County hosts an annual ‘Meat Shower’ festival, celebrating the mystery.
