A tiny republic existed where New Hampshire meets Canada. Born from a confusing 1783 treaty line,[0.5s] it declared independence. July 9, 1832, locals formed a constitution and elected officials. They taxed twice—by the US and British sheriffs—sparking outrage. Two nations sent agents.[1s] The citizens chose self‐rule. For three years they operated peacefully, trading furs and timber. But in 1835 New Hampshire militia threatened invasion. The assembly surrendered.[0.8s] Annexation followed days later. The border finally settled by the 1842 Webster‐Ashburton Treaty. Today, the town of Pittsburg marks this forgotten nation's legacy.
