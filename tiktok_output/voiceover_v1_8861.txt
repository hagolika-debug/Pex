Imagine a tiny West African war that drew a US warship. The Kru—renowned seafarers, fiercely independent coastal people of Liberia. In 1915, Liberia imposed a hut tax, sparking Kru outrage. Government forced Kru into labor, igniting deeper resistance. President Wilson sent USS Chester with rifles to back Liberia. Skirmishes erupted along the Kru coast, with gunfire echoing the sea. The war ended with Kru defeat; many were killed or imprisoned. The revolt remains a forgotten chapter of colonial resistance. Today, descendants recall the struggle for autonomy and rights. Remember the Kru’s fight—history hides countless unsung battles.
