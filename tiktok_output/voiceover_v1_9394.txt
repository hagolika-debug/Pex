In 1864, Pennsylvania’s hills erupted against the Civil War draft. Farmers dodged conscription, with 75% of draftees disappearing. Rumors spread of a secret fort armed with cannons atop North Mountain. A thousand Union soldiers marched up Fishing Creek, hunting phantom rebels. They raided farms, cornfields, even hen roosts, finding no resistance. Hundreds were arrested, many sent to Fort Mifflin, some pardoned later. The supposed fort turned out to be shingle piles and maple sap tubs. General Cadwalader labeled the whole affair a 'grand farce'. Today, a historic marker reminds us of this forgotten rebellion.
