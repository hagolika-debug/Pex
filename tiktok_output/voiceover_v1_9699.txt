Imagine convicts hijacking a government brig in 1829 Tasmania. On August 14th, the brig Cyprus anchored at remote Recherche Bay. A handful of convicts overpowered the guards and seized control. Using only three pocket knives, they built a makeshift coracle boat. They set sail north, hitting New Zealand, the Chatham Islands, then Japan. In 1830 they became the first Australian ship to reach Japan. Near Canton they scuttled the Cyprus, and many were later captured. Two mutineers were the last men hanged for piracy in Britain, 1830. Their tale inspired folk songs and recent forensic discoveries confirm it. Forgotten convicts, daring escape—history's hidden pirate saga awaits you!
