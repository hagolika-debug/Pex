A sudden storm swallowed two hundred sailors in one night. Calm waters reflected a pale, fading autumn sun. Then the sky bruised purple and the wind screamed. Waves climbed higher than lighthouses, swallowing wooden decks whole. Freezing spray turned rigging into heavy, jagged glass. Captains fought blind as compasses spun in the dark. By dawn, only splintered timber drifted across silent shores. Nature never warned them before claiming the Great Lakes.
