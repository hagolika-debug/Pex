Between Canada and New Hampshire, a tiny nation briefly emerged. The 1783 Treaty of Paris left the boundary fuzzy. Local farmers faced double taxes from both nations.[0.5s]Enough was enough. On July 9, 1832, 56 men voted for independence. They wrote a bill of rights, guaranteeing religious freedom. New Hampshire militia threatened invasion.[1s]The republic trembled. On August 5, 1835, Indian Stream surrendered to New Hampshire. The 1842 Webster‐Ashburton Treaty finally fixed the border. Today, Pittsburg, NH whispers about its forgotten republic.[1.2s]But the land remembers.
