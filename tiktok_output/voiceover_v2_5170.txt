In 1518, a woman stepped outside and refused to stop dancing. Within a week, hundreds joined her, trapped in an invisible rhythm. Their muscles tore and bones cracked, yet they could not halt. Panic-stricken leaders built wooden stages and hired musicians to cure them. But the relentless drums only fueled the deadly, unstoppable frenzy. One by one, exhausted bodies collapsed into the thick street dust. Historians blame toxic mold or a collective psychological breaking point. A chilling reminder of how fragile our minds truly are.
