A tiny nation vanished between the US and Canada, unknown to most. It began in 1832 when border confusion sparked rebellion. Residents drafted a constitution, declaring independence as the Republic of Indian Stream. Only 56 out of 59 voted for freedom.[1s] The world ignored them. They set taxes, a militia, and even a court system.[0.8s] But New Hampshire's sheriff demanded debts, threatening to invade. Tensions rose.[0.5s] Shots were never fired, but fear lingered. In 1835, New Hampshire sent militia; the republic dissolved within days. Today, the land is Pittsburg, NH, but its story lives on.
