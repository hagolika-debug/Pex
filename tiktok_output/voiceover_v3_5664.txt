In 1829, convicts hijacked the brig Cyprus off Tasmania's coast. They locked up guards, marooned the crew, and set sail into unknown. Led by William Swallow, they charted a daring route across the Pacific. First Australian convicts to reach Japan[1s], a land closed to foreigners. Japanese samurai inspected the ship, noting a strange boomerang among gifts. The brig was scuttled near Canton[0.5s], and the mutineers claimed shipwreck. Two mutineers were hanged in London, the last pirates executed. Swallow survived, returned to Tasmania, and died on the harsh Port Arthur. Their story resurfaced in 2017, proved true by Japanese archives[2s].
