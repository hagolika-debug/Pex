In the 1830s, a tiny nation emerged between the U.S. and Canada. A vague line from the 1783 Treaty of Paris left the land unclaimed. Settlers grew angry as both sides sent tax collectors demanding double payments. So they declared independence on July 9, 1832, drafting their own constitution. The Republic lasted only three years, yet it had a president, court, and militia. [2s]In 1835, New Hampshire militia threatened invasion, forcing a reluctant surrender. [1s]The tiny nation vanished, but the border dispute lingered until 1842. [0.5s]Today, the town of Pittsburg, New Hampshire marks the spot with a historic plaque. A forgotten chapter shows how borders can create whole nations overnight.
