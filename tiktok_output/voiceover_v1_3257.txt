In 1819, squatters declared Grand Island a sovereign republic. They minted their own coins and elected a charismatic governor. New York State labeled them rebels, demanding they vacate the island. Sheriff Cronk tried arresting a squatter, only to be set adrift.[1s] Winter arrived; militia gathered under Lieutenant Hodge, ready to enforce the law. At dawn, soldiers marched, reading proclamations and burning cabins.[0.5s] One defiant man claimed he’d invented perpetual motion, demanding stay. Most families were forced across the river to Canada, never returning.[1s] The ‘war’ ended, but the island’s history faded into obscurity. Today, Grand Island thrives, unaware of its rebellious past.
