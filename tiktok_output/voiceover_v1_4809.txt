In 1962, a single laugh sparked a contagious epidemic. It started at a Tanzanian boarding school, completely unprovoked. Within weeks, the uncontrollable giggles spread across entire villages. Students couldn't eat, sleep, or even focus on exams. Panic forced authorities to shut down every local school. Doctors found no virus, only mass psychological hysteria. After months, the laughter finally faded as mysteriously as it began. History proves the mind can literally infect itself.
