In 1788, Austria's massive army marched toward the Ottoman frontier. Night fell near Caransebeș, and soldiers craved warm schnapps. A drunken hussar refused to share, sparking a heated argument. A single shot rang out, and chaos erupted instantly.[1s] Units mistook each other for Turks, shouting 'Turcii!' as panic spread. Artillery fired blind, amplifying the nightmare of friendly fire.[0.5s] Emperor Joseph II, lost in the turmoil, ordered a retreat. Two days later, real Ottoman forces arrived and captured the abandoned town. The 'Battle' became a legendary blunder, reminding us of miscommunication's deadly cost.[2s]
