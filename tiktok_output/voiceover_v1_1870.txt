In 1842, an American schooner sailed to West Africa's coast. The Mary Carver stopped at Little Bereby to trade camwood. Instead of help, Bereby warriors ambushed the crew with hidden weapons. Five sailors were slain, the schooner was captured and plundered. News reached Washington, prompting a U.S. Navy punitive expedition. Commodore Matthew Perry led ships that destroyed Little Bereby later that year. The incident sparked debate over U.S. foreign policy and African trade.
