Paris starving. They filled hot air balloons with mail. One flight changed everything. 1870. Prussian army surrounds Paris. No food, no news. City cut off. Desperate, France launches 'balloon mail.' Unmanned balloons float over enemy lines with letters. Over 60 flights. Thousands of letters. Parisians' only hope to tell the world they’re alive. Famous aeronaut Henri Giffard even flies a passenger... the first woman to escape by balloon. But the Prussians capture the balloons. And inside the letters... they find something unexpected. Letters are personal pleas. Enemy prints them as propaganda. Families begging for food, lovers separated—their most intimate words turned against their own city. Balloons kept flying. Trust broken. Have you heard this? Follow for untold history.
