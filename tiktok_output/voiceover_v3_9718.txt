The 1904 Olympic marathon was a chaotic, deadly survival race. Runners faced hundred-degree heat, toxic dust, and contaminated water stations. The early leader hitched a car ride for eleven miles. The eventual winner collapsed after consuming rat poison and brandy. Another top finisher sprinted barefoot while being chased by wild dogs. Only fourteen of thirty-two starters survived the brutal, unregulated course. It forced the Olympics to finally implement global safety standards.
