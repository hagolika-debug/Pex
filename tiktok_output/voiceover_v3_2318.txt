On March 3, 1876, meat mysteriously fell from the sky. Farmers in Bath County, Kentucky, watched red chunks rain down on their fields. The Crouch family’s soap‐making porch became the epicenter of this odd storm. Scientists examined the pieces, finding muscle, cartilage, and even lung tissue. Some experts blamed vulture vomit, suggesting birds expelled their meals mid‐flight. Others proposed a cyanobacteria called Nostoc, swelling like jelly after rain. Newspapers like Scientific American and The New York Times covered the bizarre event. A preserved sample survived, stored in alcohol at Transylvania University for over a century. Today, locals celebrate the mystery with a festival honoring the raining meat.
