In 1839, a harmless honey harvest sparked a near‐war. A faulty survey misread magnetic north, blurring Missouri’s border with Iowa. Missouri tax collector entered Iowa, demanding honey taxes from a farmer. The farmer chopped three bee trees, stealing honey, ignoring Iowa’s law. Iowa’s militia arrested the Missouri sheriff, sparking a tense standoff. Both sides raised 1,000 men, armed with pitchforks and flintlocks.[1s] Winter cold and empty stomachs halted fighting; soldiers drank whiskey instead. The U.S. Supreme Court finally set the border, ending the Honey War. Today, a monument marks the line where bees once caused conflict.[0.8s]
