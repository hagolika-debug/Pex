In 1788, the Austrian army marched into a night disaster. A drunken hussar refused sharing schnapps, sparking a heated argument. One shot rang out, and confusion turned friends into enemies. Soldiers shouted 'Turks! Turks!', fearing a Ottoman attack that never arrived. Language barriers twisted commands, turning 'Halt! Halt!' into frantic 'Allah! Allah!' Panicked troops fired on each other, causing massive friendly fire casualties. Emperor Joseph II tried to stop the chaos, but was lost in darkness. Two days later, the real Ottoman forces arrived and easily captured Karánsebes. History calls it the worst friendly fire incident—an army destroyed by its own guns.
