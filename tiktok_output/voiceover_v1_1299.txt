This ‘mermaid’ fooled a nation. The truth is so much wilder. It supposedly came from Fiji, captured by ‘Dr. J. Griffin.’ Spoiler: he was a fake. Launched at London’s Royal Academy, then P.T. Barnum took it on a US tour. At the 1904 St. Louis World’s Fair, it was the star attraction. Millions paid. It wasn’t pretty. A shriveled, grinning monkey-torso sewn to a fish tail. Scientists finally examined it. Monkey head, orangutan arms, fish tail. A taxidermy scam. By St. Louis, the hoax was decades old. Barnum’s team knew it was fake. People *wanted* to believe.
