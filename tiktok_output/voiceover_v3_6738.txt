In a remote New England forest, a tiny nation sprang up. A vague 1783 border treaty left the land in limbo. Residents faced double taxes from both sides.[1s] On July 9, 1832, they declared independence, drafting a constitution. A three‐person council governed three hundred settlers, voting on daily matters. But neighboring powers ignored their claims entirely.[0.5s] New Hampshire militia marched in 1835, demanding surrender. By August 5, the Republic dissolved, becoming part of New Hampshire. The border was finally fixed in 1842 by the Webster‐Ashburton Treaty.[1s] Today, a modest marker commemorates the lost nation’s brief freedom.
