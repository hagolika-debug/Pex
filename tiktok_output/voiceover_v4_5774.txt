In 1832, 56 settlers declared independence, forming a tiny nation. Nestled between New Hampshire and British Canada, Indian Stream claimed its own borders.[0.5s] A vague 1783 treaty left the Connecticut River headwaters disputed. Residents drafted a constitution, guaranteeing religious freedom and local militias. They taxed livestock, land, and even poll taxes to fund their government. [1s]But neighboring powers ignored the fledgling state, demanding control. In 1834, New Hampshire’s sheriff tried collecting debts, sparking tension. August 4, 1835, militia threatened; the next day Indian Stream yielded. [0.8s]Today, the area is Pittsburg, New Hampshire, a quiet reminder.
