In 1904, Russian warships opened fire on British fishing boats. The exhausted Baltic Fleet navigated the North Sea at midnight. Nervous sailors spotted shadows and screamed Japanese torpedo boats were attacking. Heavy artillery erupted. Two trawlers sank instantly. Fishermen drowned in freezing waters. London demanded justice. St. Petersburg claimed self-defense. Global war seemed inevitable. An international tribunal investigated the wreckage and found only harmless nets. Russia paid massive reparations. The humiliated fleet continued its doomed voyage. One foggy night, military paranoia nearly ignited a devastating world war.
