Imagine rain falling, but it's chunks of raw meat from the sky. On March 3, 1876, farmers near Olympia Springs witnessed the oddity. Mrs. Crouch reported meat landing on her porch while she made soap. Pieces spread across a 100‐by‐50‐yard area, each about two inches wide. The event made headlines in Scientific American and The New York Times. Scientists first guessed cyanobacteria, later identifying actual animal tissue. The leading theory: startled vultures regurgitated partially digested meat overhead. In 2004, a preserved sample resurfaced at Transylvania University for study. Today, Bath County celebrates the mystery with an annual “Meat Shower” festival.
