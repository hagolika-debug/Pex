A firestorm killed thousands, yet history completely forgot it. October 1871. Wisconsin’s Peshtigo region faces a brutal drought. Clearing fires merge into a monstrous, unstoppable wall of flame. Witnesses describe a spinning fire tornado lifting houses into the sky. Over twelve hundred people perish, many drowning in the river. But Chicago’s great fire steals every newspaper headline that week. Survivors rebuild quietly, their trauma buried beneath national distraction. Today, a quiet museum honors the forgotten dead of Peshtigo. Some tragedies vanish not from lack of impact, but timing.
