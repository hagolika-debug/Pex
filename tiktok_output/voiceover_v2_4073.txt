In 1839, Iowa and Missouri almost fought over honey. A survey mistake swapped magnetic north for true north, blurring boundaries. Three bee‐laden trees were cut, sparking a tax dispute. Missouri sent Sheriff Gregory to collect taxes; Iowans seized him.[1s] Both sides mustered 1,000 militia, armed with flintlocks and pitchforks.[0.5s] They faced off at the border, but no shots fired.[0.8s] The standoff ended when winter froze their resolve and supplies. The U.S. Supreme Court finally settled the line in 1849. Today, the border remains, marked by the quirky Honey War monument.
