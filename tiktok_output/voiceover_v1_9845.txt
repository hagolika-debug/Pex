In 1901, the bustling city of Kumasi faced a hidden rebellion. British West African troops hadn't been paid for months, fueling simmering anger. When promises fell short, 60 soldiers vanished after an evening parade. On March 18, mutineers opened fire on colonial forces, sparking chaos. British troops retaliated, killing twelve mutineers as the revolt spread. Fighting lasted three weeks, with skirmishes echoing through Kumasi's streets. Eventually, the rebels were captured and taken to Sierra Leone for trial. Most were sentenced, a dozen slated for execution, later commuted. Today, the Kumasi Mutiny remains a forgotten chapter of colonial resistance.
