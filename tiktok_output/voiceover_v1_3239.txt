In 1876, a small Kentucky farm experienced a bizarre meat rain. On March 3, chunks of red meat fell over a 100‐by‐50‐yard field. Local women saw meat dropping like snow, calling it a divine sign. Scientists collected samples and sent them to labs in New York and Ohio. Analyses identified animal lung, muscle, and cartilage in the mysterious pieces. The leading theory: startled vultures regurgitated partially digested meat mid‐flight. Other ideas ranged from cyanobacteria Nostoc to cosmic meat from space. The odd event made headlines across the nation, even in The New York Times. A preserved sample now rests at Transylvania University, a reminder of strange weather.
