In 1832, a tiny nation emerged between New Hampshire and Canada. A vague 1783 Treaty line left the land unclaimed by anyone. Residents declared independence on July 9, forming the Indian Stream Republic. They drafted a constitution, elected council, and appointed a justice of peace. Both British sheriffs and American tax collectors taxed the same families twice. In 1835 New Hampshire militia marched, demanding the republic's surrender. After brief resistance, Indian Stream voted to join New Hampshire on August 5. The area became Pittsburg, New Hampshire, marked by a historic plaque today.
