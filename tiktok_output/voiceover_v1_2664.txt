A schoolgirl’s giggle sparked a contagious epidemic across an entire nation. In 1962 Tanzania, a simple classroom joke turned terrifying. Within days, hundreds of students collapsed into uncontrollable, hysterical laughter. Victims cried, fainted, and couldn’t stop for hours or days. Terrified officials closed every school to stop the mysterious outbreak. The phenomenon jumped to neighboring villages, infecting thousands of people. Doctors discovered it wasn’t a virus, but extreme collective stress. After months of chaos, the laughter finally faded into silence. A haunting reminder of how deeply our minds shape reality.
