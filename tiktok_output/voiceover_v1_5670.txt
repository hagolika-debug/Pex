A war sparked by a single pig.[0.5s] In 1859, the US and Britain disputed San Juan Islands. American farmer Lyman Cutlar shot a British pig rooting in his garden.[1s] Britain demanded Cutlar’s arrest; the US sent troops to protect settlers. Both sides landed cannons on San Juan, ready for battle. No one died—only the pig became the war’s sole casualty.[0.8s] General Winfield Scott urged calm, leading to joint occupation. In 1872, Kaiser Wilhelm I’s commission awarded the islands to the United States. Today, the Pig War reminds us how tiny sparks can ignite nations.[1s] Imagine a world where pigs decide borders—history’s quirkiest conflict.
