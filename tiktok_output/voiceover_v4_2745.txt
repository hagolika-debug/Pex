In 1795, French cavalry rode onto frozen seas. They faced the Dutch fleet, trapped in thick ice. Night of January 23rd[0.5s], hussars crossed the ice. No guns fired; only hooves whispered across the water.[1s] French negotiated surrender; fourteen ships surrendered without a shot. This bizarre battle became the only recorded cavalry capture of a fleet.[0.8s] The ice melted weeks later, freeing the ships for France. History rarely sees horses command navies; Den Helder remains legend.
