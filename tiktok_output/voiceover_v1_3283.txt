In October 1923, Hamburg trembled under a sudden communist revolt Hyperinflation crippled Germany, fueling desperation among workers and soldiers On the night of the 22nd, rebels seized police stations They raised barricades, shouting 'Soviet Germany!' across the harbor Police responded with gunfire.[1s]The streets echoed with chaos Within hours, the uprising crumbled under superior forces Seventeen police officers, twenty‐one rebels, and sixty‐one civilians died Over 1,400 were arrested.[0.8s]Trials lasted months, silencing dissent The failed revolt hardened Germany’s politics, paving the way to darker times
