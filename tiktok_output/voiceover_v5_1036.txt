In 897, Rome staged a courtroom drama unlike any other. Pope Stephen VI ordered the corpse of his predecessor, Pope Formosus, exhumed. The dead pope sat on a throne, a deacon answering for him. Stephen accused Formosus of perjury and illegal bishoprics.[1s] The corpse was found guilty; its papal vestments stripped away. Three fingers of the blessing hand were cut off, a gruesome sentence.[0.5s] The body was tossed into the Tiber, where it floated downstream. Public outrage erupted, leading to Stephen's downfall and a swift reversal. Later popes nullified the trial, restoring Formosus' honor. The Cadaver Synod remains a chilling reminder of power's deadly games.[2s]
