The 1904 Olympic marathon was a chaotic disaster history forgot. Thirty-two athletes lined up on a sweltering Missouri highway with no water stations. Support cars kicked up blinding dust, forcing competitors to swallow dirt and vomit. Fred Lorz hitched a ride for eleven miles, then casually jogged across the finish line. Thomas Hicks won after trainers fed him rat poison, raw eggs, and brandy. A Cuban runner stopped to eat rotten apples, while another was chased by wild dogs. Hicks hallucinated across the line, carried by his coaches as officials debated the winner. Lorz was disqualified, Hicks kept his gold, and the Olympics finally banned motorized pacing. It remains the strangest race ever run, proving history is often stranger than fiction.
