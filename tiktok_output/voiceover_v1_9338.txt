In 1832, a tiny New England valley declared its independence. The US and British Canada disputed a vague border in the region. Residents drafted a constitution, forming the Republic of Indian Stream. Only 56 men voted; 400 souls claimed this new nation. They created their own militia, taxes, courts, and a council. Both neighboring powers ignored the fledgling state, demanding taxes and service. In August 1835, New Hampshire threatened force; the republic surrendered. The 1842 Webster‐Ashburton Treaty finally settled the boundary dispute. Today it’s Pittsburg, New Hampshire – once a forgotten country.
