In 1839, Iowa and Missouri nearly went to war over honey. A faulty survey misplaced the border, sparking a bitter dispute. Missouri tax collector cut down three bee trees[0.5s] for unpaid taxes. Iowa officials arrested the Missouri sheriff, escalating tensions dramatically. Militias gathered, armed with pitchforks, flintlocks, and even sausage stuffers. Both sides faced off at the border, but no shots fired.[1s] The conflict was called the 'Honey War' and stayed bloodless. The U.S. Supreme Court finally settled the border[0.8s] in 1849. Today, a monument marks the spot where bees almost started a war.
