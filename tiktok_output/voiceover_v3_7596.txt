In 1958, a mountain collapsed into the ocean and birthed history’s tallest wave. Alaska’s Lituya Bay seemed peaceful until a massive earthquake shook the cliffs. Three hundred million tons of rock plunged into the narrow fjord instantly. The impact launched a megatsunami over seventeen hundred feet into the sky. Two fishing boats rode the monstrous wall of water over the ridge. They survived while everything below was stripped bare down to solid bedrock. Geologists later confirmed the impossible, proving nature’s fury defies all human limits.
