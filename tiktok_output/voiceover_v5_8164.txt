In 1839, Iowa and Missouri almost fought over honey. A strip of forest brimmed with honeybee hives, tempting settlers. Missouri sheriff crossed the border, chopping down three honey trees. Iowa militia arrested the sheriff, sparking a standoff. Both states raised militias; forty to fifty men gathered each side. Winter snow fell.[1s] No shots fired on battlefield. Leaders met in Waterloo, Missouri, negotiated a peaceful armistice. Soldiers opened a whiskey barrel.[0.8s] Celebrated with half‐split deer. The dispute finally settled by the Supreme Court in 1851.
