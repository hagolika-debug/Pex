Boston 1919:[0.8s] a sugary tsunami surged through the streets. A 50‐foot steel tank, holding 2.3 million gallons, burst. Warmer weather thinned the molasses, pressure cracked the rusted rivets. A 15‐foot wall of sticky syrup raced at 35 mph.[1s] It crushed streetcars,[0.5s] toppled buildings, and buried 21 victims. Navy cadets waded through the goo, pulling survivors from the flood. The stink lingered for decades; the site now a park. A landmark class‐action forced the company to pay millions in damages. Today, engineers study the disaster to prevent another 'Molassacre'.
