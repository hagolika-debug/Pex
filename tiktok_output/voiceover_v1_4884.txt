Winter 1795, the Zuiderzee froze solid, trapping a Dutch fleet. French Revolutionary hussars rode across the ice, a sight never before imagined. General de Winter ordered the cavalry to seize the ships without firing. The Dutch captains, ordered not to resist, surrendered their 14 warships. No cannons roared; only hooves clanged on frozen water.[1s] Fourteen ships, 850 guns, changed hands without a single shot. History calls it the only cavalry capture of a navy. The frozen fleet became a symbol of bizarre warfare. Today, the story echoes across ice, reminding us:[0.5s] war is unpredictable.
